Building a strong neural network for a trading robot involves a few key steps:

Data Collection and Preprocessing: Gather historical market data, including price movements, trading volume, news sentiment, and other relevant indicators. Clean and normalize this data so it's ready for training.

Feature Engineering: Identify and create meaningful features from your data. This could include technical indicators (like moving averages or RSI), sentiment scores, or macroeconomic data.

Model Selection: Choose a neural network architecture that suits your needs. For trading, recurrent neural networks (RNNs) or long short-term memory networks (LSTMs) are popular for capturing time series patterns. Convolutional neural networks (CNNs) can also be useful for identifying patterns in data.

Training and Validation: Split your data into training, validation, and test sets. Train your model on historical data and validate it to ensure it generalizes well. Fine-tune hyperparameters to improve performance.

Backtesting: Once trained, backtest your model on historical data it hasn't seen before to see how it would have performed in real trading scenarios.

Continuous Learning and Improvement: Markets evolve, so it's important to continually update your model with new data and re-train it periodically.
